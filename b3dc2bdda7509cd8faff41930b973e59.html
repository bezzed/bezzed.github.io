3 products that are useful for men's health

1. Grunkohl.

Curly leafy Kale contains vitamins C, E and A (in the form of beta-carotene), as well as minerals (manganese and zinc), and is extremely useful, as it is rich in antioxidants and helps with anti-inflammatory processes in the body. The plant belongs to the quince family, such as beets and spinach.

The amount of phytonutrients in cabbage is even greater than scientists initially thought, although three dozen antioxidant phytonutrients have previously been found in cabbage, including betalains (betacyanins, a also betaxanthins) and epoxy xanthophylls. Many of these antioxidant phytonutrients give curly cabbage special colors, which are colored by its roots, stems and leaf veins.

Betalin pigments in cabbage (which are also found in beets) contribute to the so-called second phase of the purification process in when the destroyed toxins are combined with other molecules, so that the body can get rid of them. Grunkol also includes important nutritional elements such as magnesium and vitamin K1, which help maintain bone strength.

In addition, this cabbage contains a flavonoid called Syringa it is also used as an acid, which has a beneficial effect on the regulation of blood sugar levels in people with diabetes, as well as kaempferol, a flavonol that can help in the fight against cancer and reduce the risk of chronic diseases, including cardiovascular ones.

2. Garlic.

Garlic is rich in selenium, calcium, manganese, phosphorus and vitamin B6 and C, which means that it benefits both our bones and our thyroid. It is believed that the main part of its medicinal properties is due to the content of sulfur in substances such as allicin, which gives off a characteristic smell.

In General, the use of garlic can be divided into 5 basic groups:

- reduces inflammation (reduces the risk of osteoarthritis, etc.);

- strengthens the immune system, has antifungal, antiviral and antiparasitic properties);

- improves cardiovascular activity and blood circulation;

- protects against clots, prevents the formation of plaque, improves the level of lipids in the body and reduces blood pressure;

- toxic to 14 different types of cancer cells (in organs such as the brain, stomach, lungs and pancreas).

In addition, garlic can be effective against drug-resistant bacteria, and according to a study on this topic, while allicin when absorbed, the body produces sulfonic acid, which interacts with dangerous free radicals faster than any other substance known to science. For this reason, garlic is included in the list of the best products that slow down aging.

3. Seedlings.

They are filled with useful information elements that protect against free radicals.

For example, fresh broccoli sprouts are much more nutritious than broccoli itself, because they do not need to be eaten in large quantities. Studies show that some seedlings that are stored for three days contain 10-100 times more glucoraphanin - a chemoprotective compound found in ripe broccoli. Glucoraphanin also protects against the effects of toxic pollutants, improving the body's ability to get rid of them. It is also known to protect against cancer.

If you have never paid attention to them, you can start by trying broccoli sprouts, watercress or sunflower seeds.
