5 special products for men's health

There are several types of products that men should never neglect. Even a small amount of them can quickly improve nutrition, help build muscle mass, and stimulate metabolism, boost immunity and protect the heart. Make sure to make room for them on your plate.

1. Seedlings.

Sprouts, in which the germination process has begun but not finished, contain three times as much soluble fiber, which is a key element in controlling blood sugar levels (USC). In addition, they are a source of digestive enzymes, beneficial intestinal bacteria, and antioxidants. Sprouts contain fiber and protein and are characterized by a high concentration of some valuable vitamins and minerals. Carbohydrates contained in sprouts have a low glycemic index and help maintain a stable USC.

2. Dried plums.

Prunes contain two unique phytonutrients called neochlorogenic and chlorogenic acids. They protect brain cells from the damaging effects of free radicals, and help prevent chronic and cardiovascular diseases CVD) and stop the oxidation of bad cholesterol (LDL). The combination of soluble fiber and carbohydrates in prunes supports a healthy USC. These dried fruits reduce the risk of osteoporosis by preventing bone loss and promoting bone recovery. A high concentration of vitamin C increases the immune system and stimulates formation of collagen, a necessary element for strong and healthy tissues.

3. Black mussels.

Black mussels are a great food for muscles because they are rich in protein - just one serving contains about 20 g of protein. At the same time, they are low in fat, and they are considered a great addition to a weight-loss regime. Mussels are a rich source of many essential vitamins and minerals, such As b vitamins, vitamin C, folic acid, iron, phosphorus, manganese, selenium and zinc. Vitamin B12 is essential for proper functioning of the nervous system, formation of red blood cells, and proper growth and development of tissues. Selenium supports immune system and thyroid function. Manganese is a necessary element for bone health and energy metabolism. Mussels are a source of omega-3s, which support heart health and reduce the risk of CVD.

4. Macadamia nuts.

These delicious nuts contain 17 grams of monounsaturated fat per serving portions (the most useful for the heart) and the quality of fat surpass almonds and walnuts. Monounsaturated fats help reduce excess weight by stimulating fat metabolism and limiting their storage in the body. At the same time, nuts are extremely beneficial for the health of hair, nails and skin. Macadamia contains a high dose of phosphorus, a key element for bone mineralization and proper nutrient absorption.

5. Beets.

One Cup of leaves of this plant provides more than 4 times the recommended daily intake of vitamin K, a substance that prevents hardening of the arteries. Beets contain a minimum of it is high in calories and fat, but is a rich source of vitamin C, vitamin K, b vitamins and vitamin A. the Plant is rich in important minerals including copper, calcium, sodium, potassium, iron, manganese and phosphorus. Because of this excellent nutritious part of the beet leaves provide control over the USK, strengthen bones and teeth, improve vision and stimulate the nervous system.
