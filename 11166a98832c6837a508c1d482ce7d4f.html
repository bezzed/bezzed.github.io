Which alcohol reduces blood pressure, and which increases it?

Which alcohol reduces blood pressure, and which increases it - it is important to know for those who have problems with high or low blood pressure.

Today alcoholic beverages are an integral part of the festive table. So that the holiday does not lead to negative consequences, people suffering from hypotension or hypertension should be careful in choosing alcohol.

Hypotension.

People with hypotension should carefully monitor what alcoholic beverages are available they are consumed because alcohol has a significant effect on the cardiovascular system, including blood PRESSURE. Therefore, a properly selected alcoholic drink can brighten up the evening, without harming your health.

Hypotonic patients are not recommended to drink acidic, fruit drinks. Therefore, those who should not reduce their tax base HOWEVER, it is recommended to choose drinks that contain a large amount of sugar. Sweet alcoholic beverages contribute to an increase in blood PRESSURE, so they will not harm hypotensive helpers. You can also drink strong drinks: cognac, whiskey, sweet wine.

Hypertension.

There are several factors that provoke hypertension:

- Smoking, alcohol;

- overweight or obese;

- diabetes;

- long history of Smoking;

- high cholesterol and age 55-65 years.

Most often, the most dangerous factor in increasing blood PRESSURE for people with hypertension is the use of alcohol. Therefore, hypertensive patients should be extremely careful with the use of alcoholic beverages.

Special care when choosing an alcoholic beverage should be paid to those who have hypertension caused by diabetes. People with hypertension should avoid using high-grade alcoholic beverages drinks'.

Alcohol by itself increases blood PRESSURE and in combination with sugar can become a deadly mixture for people with high blood PRESSURE. Sweet wine and vermouth significantly increase blood PRESSURE, so people with hypertension should stop using them. In addition, beer in combination with something salty can have a negative influence the health of hypertensive patients.

Strong alcoholic beverages, such as cognac or whiskey, also significantly increase blood PRESSURE. These drinks are contraindicated for those who have other problems with the cardiovascular system.

An undesirable increase or decrease in blood PRESSURE can indicate serious health problems health. Therefore, those who suffer from hypertension or hypotension should determine the cause of this phenomenon and remember that to prevent possible spikes in blood PRESSURE, you should not drink alcohol.

People with hypertension should first avoid drinking beverages that increase blood PRESSURE: coffee, sweet alcohol, energy drinks and more.

The enemy of hypertension is temperature, so staying in the sun, in the bathroom and stuffy rooms can negatively affect your health. In addition, patients with hypertension should monitor their physical activity, as it affects the work of the heart and the entire body cardiovascular system.
